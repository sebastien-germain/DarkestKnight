## SPEC-03: Position System (Darkest Dungeon Ranks)
[v8] **Global Duration Definition (canonical — summarized in [00_INDEX.md](./00_INDEX.md)):** Throughout this specification suite, "Duration: 1 turn" means the effect persists from the moment of activation until the start of the activating combatant's next turn. The effect is active during all other combatants' turns in between (including enemy turns). This applies globally to all duration references: Warrior Type OD bonus, Ghost stealth, Shrine, NanoC effects, Barrage Def/React reduction, and any other "1 turn" duration.
[v8] **Global Math Rules (canonical — summarized in [00_INDEX.md](./00_INDEX.md)):** All divisions round down (floor). Divide-then-subtract order. Successive doubling = tripling (not ×4). Hit threshold: successes must strictly exceed Defense (melee) or Reaction (ranged) — equal = miss.
### Purpose
[v4] MAJOR REWRITE. Manages P1–P4 linear rank track with Darkest Dungeon-style single-occupancy positioning. Each rank holds exactly one combatant.
### Track Layout
KNIGHT SIDE                    ENEMY SIDE
[Rank 4] [Rank 3] [Rank 2] [Rank 1]  |  [Rank 1] [Rank 2] [Rank 3] [Rank 4]
  Back     Mid-B    Mid-F    Front   |   Front    Mid-F    Mid-B     Back

### [v4] Core Rank Rules
**1 combatant per rank**. No exceptions. 4 ranks per side = max 4 combatants per side.  
**Knight squad:** 4 knights selected from 8-knight roster at mission start. See §Squad Selection below for full rules.  
**Enemy cap:** Max 4 individual enemies on-track. Reinforcements only spawn into empty ranks.  
**[v2] Bandes are OFF-TRACK.** They have no rank. Débordement hits all knights regardless.  
**Gap formula:** Gap = (attackerRank − 1) + (targetRank − 1). Min = 0.

### [v6] Cover System
[v6] NEW — Critical C3 resolution. Cover represents terrain features (walls, barricades, debris) that provide protection against ranged attacks.

**Cover Flag:**
* Each rank on either side can have a **hasCover: boolean** flag, set by encounter data (EnemySpawn ScriptableObject) or generated by NanoC Cover Wall ability (SPEC-17).
* Encounter designers specify which ranks start with cover via the encounter definition (SPEC-22).

**Cover Bonuses & Penalties:**
* A combatant in a Cover rank gains **+3 Reaction** against all incoming ranged attacks.
* A combatant **attacking with a ranged weapon from a Cover rank** suffers **-3 dice** on their attack roll (restricted firing angles).
* Cover has **no effect** on melee (contact) attacks — Defense is unaffected.
* Cover bonuses stack with Rank Modifiers (e.g., Rank 1 Cover = +3 Reaction from cover, +0 from rank = +3 total Reaction bonus).

**Weapon Effect Interactions:**
* **Tir en Sécurité:** Knight firing from a Cover rank does **NOT** suffer the -3 dice penalty. Cover Reaction bonus still applies. This represents careful, defensive firing from cover.
* **Artillerie:** Ignores the target's Cover Reaction bonus (+3 => +0). [v8.9] Artillerie fires over/around cover — it does NOT hit or damage the cover itself. Even when future destructible cover is implemented, Artillerie bypasses cover entirely (hits the combatant, not the terrain).
* **Dispersion:** Cover does not protect against Dispersion damage if the combatant is hit as a secondary target.

[v8.9] **Cover Persistence & Destruction:**
* **Encounter cover** (set by EnemySpawn data): persists for the entire encounter. **Indestructible in demo.** Future content: destructible cover will require a dedicated design pass (HP, damage thresholds, debris effects). Not in demo scope.
* **NanoC Cover Wall** (SPEC-17.3): temporary, expires after 10 turns. On expiry or Priest Fold/death: `hasCover` is removed from the rank. See §Cover Removal Procedure below.
* **Enemies cannot destroy cover in demo.** No enemy capacity targets cover objects. Future content may add siege-type enemies.

**Cover Removal Procedure** (triggered when any cover source is removed from a rank):
1. Set `rank.hasCover = false` on the affected rank.
2. For each combatant at that rank: if their `activeStyle == MISE_A_COUVERT`, force switch to `Standard`. Fire `OnStyleChanged(combatant, STANDARD)`.
3. Combatants using any other style (Standard, Agressif, Défensif, etc.) are **unaffected** — do not change their style.
4. Fire `OnCoverRemoved(rank)` event (SPEC-27) for UI/VFX.

**Enemy Cover:**
Cover rules are **symmetrical** — enemy combatants in a Cover rank gain the same bonuses and suffer the same penalties as knights:
* **+3 Reaction** against incoming ranged attacks (makes them harder to hit at range).
* **−3 dice** penalty when firing from Cover (restricted firing angles).
* Contact attacks are unaffected by cover (Defense unchanged).

Enemy AI behavior with cover:
* **HOLD** enemies prefer staying in cover ranks when available.
* **ADVANCE** enemies may leave cover to close distance to melee range.
* Enemies do not have Tir en Sécurité (no demo enemy bypasses the −3 firing penalty from cover).

### [v4] Movement — DD Rank Swap System
Movement in the rank system works like Darkest Dungeon: knights swap positions with adjacent allies or shift into empty slots.

Move into empty adjacent rank: 1 Movement Action. Knight shifts one rank forward or back into empty slot.
Swap with adjacent ally: 1 Movement Action. Knight and ally exchange ranks simultaneously. No action cost for the swapped ally.
Move 2 ranks: Spend Combat Action on a second movement (free substitution per SPEC-04 §Action Economy). Can chain two single-rank moves or swaps.
Cannot pass through occupied enemy ranks. Only ally swaps and empty slots.

### [v4] Forced Displacement
Forced displacement pushes target 1+ ranks toward their back line.
Chain-swap rule: If target is pushed into an occupied ally rank, those two swap. The displaced ally is NOT further pushed.
Push into empty rank: Target simply moves. No chain.
Push past Rank 4: Clamped to Rank 4. No further effect.
Colonne Vertébrale Brisée: [v8.9] See [SPEC-23](./08_ContentData.md) Column 3, Row 1 for canonical definition. Summary: movement costs 2 actions per rank shift, cannot Sprint, forced displacement still applies.
Jambe Mutilée: 1 rank of movement costs 2 Movement Actions.

### [v6] Death and Empty Ranks
When a combatant dies, their rank becomes empty.
Adjacent combatants may shift into the empty rank on their turn (1 Movement Action).
Enemy reinforcements spawn into empty enemy ranks only. If all 4 enemy ranks occupied, reinforcements are queued. [v8] Queue check: triggers at the start of the enemy phase, before any enemy acts. Queued enemies spawn into ranks vacated by deaths in the preceding knight phase. [v8.9] **Reinforcement initiative:** On spawn, the reinforcement rolls initiative per SPEC-15 (3D6 + Initiative score, or fixed value for special enemies). The reinforcement is inserted into the current round's queue at its rolled initiative value. If the rolled initiative is higher than the current position in the queue (i.e., the round hasn't passed that initiative slot yet), the reinforcement acts this round. If lower (initiative slot already passed this round), it waits until the next round. [v8.9] **Spawn priority:** Reinforcement spawn order follows the `enemyIdsInOrder` list from the EncounterSO (SPEC-29). The content designer controls the order. Tier-based priority (highest-tier first) is used ONLY when multiple reinforcements can spawn simultaneously into multiple empty ranks in the same check — in that case, higher-tier enemies from the queue take precedence. If tied, random.
Knight death leaves rank empty permanently for that encounter (no mid-mission roster swap).

### [v4] Weapon Rank Targeting
Weapons have rank restrictions derived from the Gap formula. A weapon with Max Gap X can only be used when `(attackerRank − 1) + (targetRank − 1) ≤ X`.

**Important:** Valid targeting depends on the combination of attacker AND target rank — it cannot be reduced to independent "usable from" and "can target" columns without losing accuracy. The validation table below shows all legal combinations.

#### Gap Matrix (all attacker × target rank combinations)

| | Enemy R1 | Enemy R2 | Enemy R3 | Enemy R4 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Knight R1** | 0 | 1 | 2 | 3 |
| **Knight R2** | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 |
| **Knight R3** | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 |
| **Knight R4** | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 |

#### Valid Targeting per Range Band

| Range Band | Max Gap | Valid Attacker → Target Combinations |
|---|---|---|
| Contact | 1 | K1→E1, K1→E2, K2→E1 |
| Courte | 2 | K1→E1/E2/E3, K2→E1/E2, K3→E1 |
| Moyenne | 4 | All **except** K3→E4, K4→E3, K4→E4 |
| Longue | 5 | All **except** K4→E4 |
| Lointaine | 6 | All combinations |

#### Simplified Summary (approximate — use gap check for authoritative validation)

| Range Band | Usable From (Knight Ranks) | Can Target (Enemy Ranks) | Restriction |
|---|---|---|---|
| Contact | Rank 1–2 | Rank 1–2 | Only if gap ≤ 1 (e.g., K2 can only reach E1) |
| Courte | Rank 1–3 | Rank 1–3 | Only if gap ≤ 2 (e.g., K3 can only reach E1) |
| Moyenne | Rank 1–4 | Rank 1–4 | K3 cannot reach E4; K4 cannot reach E3/E4 |
| Longue | Rank 1–4 | Rank 1–4 | K4 cannot reach E4 |
| Lointaine | Rank 1–4 | Rank 1–4 | No restriction — all combos valid |

**Implementation note:** Always use the gap formula for authoritative validation: `if ((attackerRank - 1) + (targetRank - 1) <= weapon.maxGap) → valid`. The simplified summary is for human reference only.

Note: Contact weapons at Rank 1 can hit enemy Rank 1 (gap=0) or Rank 2 (gap=1). A Warrior at Rank 2 can melee enemy Rank 1 (gap=1) but NOT enemy Rank 2 (gap=2). A knight at Rank 3+ cannot melee at all.

### [v4] Squad Selection (Mission Start)
Before each mission, the player selects 4 knights from the 8-knight roster. Selection is final for the entire mission run. Remaining 4 knights are unavailable but retain their state (injuries, resources) for future missions.  
Selection screen: Show all 8 knights with current PS, PA, PE, injuries, abilities.  
Composition guidance: UI suggests balanced team (1 melee, 1 tank, 1 support, 1 flex).  
Lock: Player confirms. Selected 4 proceed to deployment phase.  

### [v3] Deployment Phase (C3 Resolved)
Before the first turn of each encounter, the player places the 4 selected knights on Ranks 1–4.  
Enemy placement: Ranks loaded from encounter data (EnemySpawn ScriptableObject).  
Knight placement: Player assigns each knight to a rank. 1 knight per rank. All 4 must be placed.  
Default suggestion: UI pre-places Warrior at Rank 1, Paladin at Rank 2, Priest at Rank 3, Rogue at Rank 4.  
Confirm: Player presses ‘Deploy.’ Initiative rolls. Combat begins.  

### Acceptance Criteria
[v4] Rank 1 vs Rank 1: Gap=0. Contact weapon succeeds.  
[v4] Rank 3 vs Rank 1: Gap=2+0=2. Contact (maxGap 1) rejected. Courte (maxGap 2) succeeds.  
[v4] Knight at Rank 2 moves to empty Rank 1: 1 Movement Action. Knight now at Rank 1.  
[v4] Knight at Rank 1 swaps with ally at Rank 2: both exchange. 1 Movement Action for initiator only.  
[v4] Enemy pushes knight from Rank 1 to Rank 2 (occupied by ally): knight and ally swap. Ally now at Rank 1.  
[v4] Knight dies at Rank 2: rank becomes empty. No replacement available mid-mission.  
[v4] 4 enemy ranks full, reinforcement queued: spawns when next enemy dies.  
Colonne Vertébrale Brisée: movement costs 2 actions per rank shift, forced displacement still applies. See [SPEC-23](./08_ContentData.md).  


[v6] Artillerie weapon vs enemy in Cover rank: ignore +3 Reaction bonus. Enemy Reaction = base value.

## SPEC-15: Turn Queue & Initiative
### Purpose
Turn order management. FFX-style timeline. [v4] M1 resolved: initiative fully specified. DD rank movement integrated.

### [v4] Initiative Roll (M1 Resolved)
When: Once at the start of each encounter. Never re-rolled.
Formula: 3D6 + Initiative score. Results persist for entire combat.
Trop prudent disadvantage: 2D6 instead of 3D6.
Bandes: Always initiative 1 (no roll).
Masque Majeur enemies: Always initiative 30 (no roll).
Ties: Higher derived Initiative score first. If still tied, random.

### [v4] Delay Initiative
A knight may delay to a lower initiative value (announced at start of their turn).
Delay applies to entire turn (both actions). Cannot split.

NPCs: only Patron-type enemies may delay.

### [v4] Turn Structure (DD Ranks)
Each turn: 1 Combat Action + 1 Movement Action, in either order. **For the complete action economy table (all action costs, style costs, restrictions), see [SPEC-04](./03_CombatRolls.md) §Action Economy (canonical).** For Nod targeting rules including Agony exceptions, see [SPEC-33](./13_NodSystem.md) §33.3 (canonical).
A Combat Action may be spent on any Movement-cost action (free substitution, no declaration needed — see SPEC-04 §Action Economy for canonical rules). This gives up to 2 movement-type actions per turn but no attack.
[v4] Movement in DD ranks: Move into empty adjacent rank OR swap with adjacent ally = 1 Movement Action. See SPEC-03.
Free actions: 1 per type per turn (Ghost activation, style switch, weapon draw, etc.).

### Acceptance Criteria
[v4] Initiative rolled at encounter start. Same values used on turns 2, 3, etc.
[v4] Knight delays from 15 to 8: acts at position 8 in queue. Next turn can act at 15 or delay again.
Bande always last (init 1). Masque Majeur always first (init 30).
